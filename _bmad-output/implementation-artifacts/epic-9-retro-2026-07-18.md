## What Went Well

- **Dashboard / Driver experience coheres.** Work-mode strip, Next Steps, satisfaction glance, and follow-up surfaces guide daily work rather than only reflecting finished artifacts (9.8, 9.9).
- **Deferred and follow-ups are visible throughout the app.** Sunburst peers, Unplanned root, sprint Unplanned lane, scan-first lists, detail deep-links, filtered group pages (9.6–9.13).
- **Visibility changes behavior.** Project Lead confirmed deferred items are starting to get addressed and resolved now that they appear in primary attention surfaces — Epic 8's "invisible commitments" gap closed in practice.
- **Split-don't-absorb + compose later stories.** 9.9 composes 9.1–9.3; 9.10/9.11 overlay 9.6 chrome without destroying provenance; 9.13 finishes the 9.12 temporary href seam.
- **Truthfulness under pressure.** Unmapped vs Deferred (never color-only); honest empty evidence pills; ledger-agreed counts; conservative heuristics (false negatives over false positives).

## Challenges

- **Sunburst and dashboard experimentation was messy.** Geometry evolved across 9.7 → 9.12 → 9.13; getting consistency between project/epic sunbursts and related screens was difficult.
- **Epic grew mid-flight (6 → 13).** Product-right correct-course appends stacked a second arc (follow-up geometry/navigation) onto the first (traceability + review ergonomics).
- **Silhouette re-locks at review.** Locked intent still moved when cross-screen membership/click contracts weren't frozen early enough.
- **Heuristic tax.** Free-text inference for evidence, deferral, slugs, and attribution produced recurring review patches (copy-payload/linkify traps, href depth, phantom/orphan epics).

## Key Insights

1. **Visibility in the Driver's primary geometry is what makes debt burn down** — side lists alone do not.
2. **Primary attention surfaces need a locked membership + click-destination contract early**, or every sibling story becomes another consistency pass.
3. **Correct-course can be product-right and still argue for a second epic boundary** when appends roughly double story count (Matthew: "perhaps it should have been two epics").
