## Challenges

- **Story 7.1 absorbed two full redesign rounds after review**, not before it. The relationship-graph pivot and the two-column/syntax-highlighting round were both structural, not cosmetic — exactly the class of rework Epic 3's retrospective already flagged ("elicit visual intent at create-story," still an open action item from Epic 3). This is the third epic in a row where a new visual surface got corrected after the fact rather than having its direction elicited up front.
- **Epic 6 Retrospective Action Item #3 (every net-new epic verifies cross-surface reach before dev starts) was not applied to Epic 7 or, until this retrospective, to Epic 8.** Epic 7's stories were already fully drafted before Epic 6's retro landed the rule (fair — no window existed). But Epic 8's seven stories were also already drafted before Epic 6's retro, and by the time this retrospective ran, they were still sitting `ready-for-dev` with none started — a real, open window that had gone unused. **The deeper issue the discussion surfaced:** by the time any epic's retrospective happens, the next epic's stories are essentially always already drafted (Epic 8's were seeded 2026-07-09, three days before Epic 6's retro even ran). So "every net-new epic opens with an integration spike" has no natural trigger point in this project's actual workflow — there is no moment where an epic has zero stories and a retro fires in between. The rule's *wording* was the bug, not the team's follow-through.
- **The `epics.md` ↔ `sprint-status.yaml` reconciliation (Epic 6 Action #1) did land** (verified this retro — `epics.md`'s Epic 6 section now correctly shows the 6.3 spike redirect and Stories 6.8–6.12), but Epic 6 Action #4 (`KnownIndexGroups` omitting `adrs`/`retros`) and Action #5 (F5-in-real-VS-Code smoke) remain open, carried a second epic now.

## Key Insights

1. **"Opens with" was the wrong verb for a rule about story sequencing in a workflow where planning runs ahead of execution.** The corrected form — "before the first story in a net-new epic moves to `in-progress`" — names the actual, real trigger point: the moment between drafting and starting dev, which can arrive well after the epic's retro-eligible predecessor has already closed.
2. **A process rule needs to survive being checked against the *next* thing sitting in front of the team, not just the next *hypothetical* epic.** The first pass at reviewing Action #3 reached for "wait for Epic 11-18, those haven't been created yet" — a comfortable answer that avoided the uncomfortable one: Epic 8 was sitting right there, undone, the whole time.
3. **Visual-intent elicitation (Epic 3's still-open action item) keeps recurring because create-story doesn't yet have a forcing function for it.** Three epics running (3, 6, 7) have each had at least one story redesigned post-review rather than pre-elicited. This is now a pattern robust enough to warrant treating as a create-story process gap, not a one-off.
